Why is it part of morality?

Morality is following the principles concerning the distinction between right and wrong or good and bad behavior.

  • It is related to human dignity

    • Recognizing human dignity means recognizing every human has value and worth. Denying equality diminishes a person's inherent value, which is morally wrong.

  • It is related to compassion and empathy

    • Compassion and empathy are both moral virtues. Being a misogynist makes someone biased toward men, while being a misandrist makes a person biased toward women. If one is biased towards a certain gender, it hinders them from giving someone the compassion and empathy they deserve.

  • Being against gender equality means treating people unfairly

    • If morality is based on the good beliefs and ideas that are agreed upon by the majority of people, wouldn’t treating people unfairly for a certain trait that isn’t even bad go against the belief that we should treat people fairly?

  • Moral people have the responsibility and willingness to oppose injustice

    • Injustice, by definition, is a lack of fairness or justice. Being against gender equality makes you lack in those two things. How can someone call themself a moral person then?
